Concrete Ramps & Drainage: Do It Once, Right

Smart grading and details that keep water away—and customers safe.

May 15, 2025 • 4–5 min read

Why drainage comes first

Standing water shortens pavement life and creates hazards. Before adding ramps or sidewalks, confirm slopes carry water to inlets or swales—and that soil won’t wash out.

Details that matter

  • Ramp placement: Align with accessible routes to doors, not traffic lanes.
  • Landings: Level, properly sized, and clear of obstructions.
  • Transitions: Tie-in to asphalt without creating ponding or trip edges.
  • Detectable warnings: Specify durable materials at curb ramps.

Typical scope

  1. Demo and excavation; confirm subgrade and compaction.
  2. Set forms to grade and slope; place reinforcement as needed.
  3. Pour, finish, edge; cure per spec; sawcut joints.
  4. Stripe or add signage after cure; verify accessible routes.

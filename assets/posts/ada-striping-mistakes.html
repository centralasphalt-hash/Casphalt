ADA & Striping: Common Layout Mistakes

The details that keep you compliant—and customers safe.

June 3, 2025 • 4–6 min read

Frequent issues we correct

  • Accessible route not continuous: Gaps or curb conflicts between stalls and entrances.
  • Slopes too steep: Stalls and access aisles over ADA slope limits.
  • Wrong stall counts: Total accessible stalls and van stalls not matching site totals.
  • Signage height/placement: Signs missing, too low, or set in wheel stops.
  • Faded markings: Illegible symbols and borders create risk.

Layout best practices

  1. Place accessible stalls closest to compliant entrances.
  2. Ensure clear, level access aisles with crosshatching.
  3. Use durable paint and proper symbol dimensions.
  4. Verify sign posts, heights, and van designations.
  5. Check curb ramps, landings, and detectable warnings.

Refresh vs. re-layout

Sometimes fresh paint is enough. Other times, a full re-layout fixes spacing and routing problems. We’ll walk the site, measure slopes, and propose the right scope.

Asphalt Repairs: Potholes vs. Overlays

Not sure whether to patch or pave? Use condition and traffic to choose the right fix.

July 12, 2025 • 5–6 min read

Overview

Repairs fall into two big buckets: patching (cut-out or skin patch) and overlay (a new layer over large areas). The right choice depends on base condition, drainage, and how much fatigue cracking you see.

When patching makes sense

  • Isolated failures (potholes, utility cuts, bad joints).
  • Sound base after sawcut — subgrade is firm, not pumping water.
  • Budget or access constraints limit area-wide work.

When overlay is better

  • Widespread alligator cracking or raveling across large areas.
  • Surface is thin/oxidized but base is mostly stable.
  • You want a uniform, like-new ride and appearance.

Don’t pave over problems

Overlays reflect cracks if active movement or base failure is left beneath. We typically patch structural spots first, then overlay.

Decision matrix (quick guide)

  • Localized + base OK → cut-out patch.
  • Localized + base soft/wet → patch + base remediation.
  • Area-wide aging + base OK → overlay after crack sealing/leveling.
  • Poor drainage → fix grades/drains first, or repairs won’t last.

Typical scope

  1. Sawcut and remove failed asphalt.
  2. Inspect base; add/compact rock if needed.
  3. Tack coat edges; install hot mix asphalt; compact to grade.
  4. For overlay: mill transitions, level course, final lift, then stripe.

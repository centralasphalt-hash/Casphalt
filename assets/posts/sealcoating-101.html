Sealcoating 101: When, Why, and How to Budget

Timing, materials, and scheduling tips for a smooth project and longer pavement life.

August 20, 2025 • 5–7 min read

Overview

Sealcoating protects asphalt from sun, water, and chemicals, restores rich black color, and helps minimize long-term costs. The key is choosing the right timing and product, and bundling related work so you don’t bring crews out twice.

When to Sealcoat

  • New pavement: First seal typically at 12–18 months after paving (once oils have cured).
  • Existing lots: Every 3–5 years, depending on traffic, weather exposure, and prior maintenance.
  • Season & cure: Aim for dry weather with daytime highs 60–95°F and allow 24–48 hours to reopen fully.

Signs It’s Time

  • Graying surface, oxidation, or hairline cracking.
  • Loss of fines/aggregate (surface looks rough or dry).
  • Faded striping and increased staining from oils.

Material Options (What to Ask About)

Different formulas balance durability, appearance, VOC/odor, and cost. Your estimator should recommend based on traffic, fuel exposure, and goals.

  • Refined coal tar emulsions: Very resistant to fuels and chemicals; dark finish.
  • Asphalt emulsions (modiﬁed): Lower odor/VOC; great appearance; popular near storefronts and schools.
  • Additives & sand loads: Improve wear, skid, and color; may affect cure and price.

Build a Smart Scope

  1. Prep: Power blow/clean, edge and weed removal, treat oil spots, repair potholes as needed.
  2. Crack sealing: Hot-applied rubberized sealant on active cracks before sealcoating.
  3. Sealcoat: One or two coats per spec, plus proper dry times and barricades.
  4. Striping: Re-layout, ADA, fire lanes, arrows, stencils, and any signage.

How to Budget (Simple Framework)

  • Map your site: Square footage, high-traffic areas, fuel exposure, shade/irrigation.
  • Bundle work: Combine crack sealing + sealcoat + striping to reduce mobilizations.
  • Phase access: Schedule in sections to keep tenants and customers moving.
  • Plan a cycle: Add a line item every 3–5 years in your annual budget.

Typical Timeline

  1. Site walk & proposal: 1–3 days after request.
  2. Scheduling: Pick weather windows and phases; notify tenants.
  3. Prep & crack seal: Same day or day prior to sealing (site-dependent).
  4. Sealcoat & stripe: Usually 1–2 days for small/medium lots.
  5. Reopen: 24–48 hours after final coat/striping, per cure and weather.

Quick FAQ

Will sealcoating fix structural issues? No—sealcoat is protective. Structural problems (base failure, deep potholes) need asphalt repairs before sealing.

How long does it last? Often 3–5 years, but traffic, weather, and prep quality matter.

Will it be slippery? Proper sand loading and additive choices maintain traction.
